Popular Destinations

Banff

Canada's first National Park offers majestic mountains and vibrant jewel-toned lakes...

Canada's first National Park offers majestic mountains, vibrant jewel-toned lakes, and untouched wilderness. This breathtaking scenery invites visitors to enjoy outdoor activities throughout the year. In summer, explore unspoiled forests and paddle across turquoise lakes, while winter invites you to ski down snowy slopes and relax in steaming hot springs. The charming mountain towns of Banff and Lake Louise also provide a warm and welcoming retreat for travelers.

Canmore

Nestled among the snow-capped peaks of the Canadian Rockies...

Nestled among the snow-capped peaks of the Canadian Rockies and just minutes from Banff National Park's eastern entrance, this historic mining town has earned a reputation as a haven for winter sports enthusiasts, particularly those heading to the cross-country skiing trails at Canmore Nordic Centre Provincial Park. In recent years, Canmore has transformed into a year-round destination, offering warm-weather adventures like hiking, mountain biking, and whitewater rafting. Set along the scenic Bow River, Canmore boasts a walkable downtown brimming with charming boutiques, art galleries, and Canada's most enchanting bookshop. With three breweries, two distilleries, and a vibrant food scene, it has become a culinary hotspot drawing diners from across Alberta.

Drumheller

A charming town nestled in the heart of the Canadian Badlands...

A charming town nestled in the heart of the Canadian Badlands, Drumheller is famously known as the Dinosaur Capital of the World. It's a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the Badlands and uncovering the prehistoric wonders that once roamed this fascinating region.
